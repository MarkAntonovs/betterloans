Shortlist: who to check first

Start with lenders that consistently approve first-time borrowers and publish clear terms on their website. Look for minimum documents (NIC + active bank account), app-based onboarding and typical decision in minutes. Many providers offer intro 0% interest for the first loan — conditions vary (amount and term caps, on-time repayment required).

What to compare

  • Amount & term — typical range Rs. 5,000–200,000 and 30–180 days. Longer terms can mean higher total cost.
  • Decision time — some decisions in ~10–30 minutes (varies by KYC and traffic).
  • Fees & APR — in Sri Lanka lenders usually show daily/weekly fees; compare the total to repay.
  • Extensions — whether you can extend and at what cost.
  • Support — live chat, hotline, language options.

Eligibility & documents

  • Age usually 18–60 (varies by lender).
  • Valid Sri Lankan NIC (front & back photos).
  • Active bank account in your name for disbursement and repayment.
  • Reachable phone & internet access; sometimes proof of income.

Fees & interest

Check the sample calculation before you apply. Intro 0% offers normally cover the first loan and only if you repay on time. Late payments trigger penalties and collection calls. Always read the lender’s T&Cs.

How to apply online

  1. Choose a licensed provider and open the application form.
  2. Enter accurate personal data and upload NIC photos.
  3. Verify phone, bank account and complete KYC checks.
  4. Wait for decision — many providers answer the same day.

Approval tips

  • Use your real information; mismatches cause rejections.
  • Keep your phone reachable for verification calls.
  • Start with smaller limits and build a positive repayment history.

FAQ

Are online loans legal in Sri Lanka? Yes, but always use licensed providers and review their terms carefully.

Do I need a bank account? Yes, lenders disburse to an active account in your name.
