Online Loans in Sri Lanka — Beginner’s Guide

Eligibility, documents, KYC checks and practical tips to get approved faster.

On this page
How the process works Eligibility Required documents Decision & disbursement Repayment & extensions Approval tips FAQ

Always review the lender’s terms and make sure the total to repay is affordable. Late payments damage your credit and add penalties.

How the process works

  1. Choose a licensed provider and open the application.
  2. Submit personal data, NIC photos and bank details.
  3. Complete KYC (SMS code, liveness check, phone call).
  4. Receive a decision; if approved, money goes to your bank account.

Eligibility

Age commonly 18–60, resident in Sri Lanka, valid NIC and an active bank account in your name. Providers may also check phone SIM ownership and device signals to reduce fraud.

Required documents

  • NIC (front & back);
  • Bank account details for disbursement;
  • Sometimes payslips/screenshots as proof of income.

Decision & disbursement

First-time applications may take longer due to manual checks. Typical range is ~10–60 minutes; some approvals are same-day only during business hours. Disbursement speed depends on your bank cut-off times.

Repayment & extensions

Know the due date and available channels (bank transfer, app, card). Extensions are offered by some lenders for a fee — check the full cost before requesting.

Approval tips

  • Use consistent personal data across NIC, bank and application.
  • Answer verification calls; keep your phone nearby.
  • Start small, repay on time, then request higher amounts.

FAQ

Do providers run credit checks? Processes vary by lender; many rely on internal risk models based on your application and device signals.
